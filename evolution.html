Evolution

April 28, 2026

We’ve got about a week left, but I’m elated to report that April has been better than March. It’s the classic “be careful what you wish for” in how this month varies from the previous because there are almost too many avenues my thoughts can wander down this month.

A brief interlude into my monthly writings to give some insight into the “process”: I tend to flag a thought or occurrence that sticks with me for longer than 24hrs and form writing around it. Not a ton of deep research (yet) as that seems a little stale and formulaic. I say that as a total cop-out, I could definitely devote more time to research but this is more fun.

Back to April:

I have a few ideas I’ll squirrel away but coming off a team offsite where I got to experience what some proclaim is “west coast, best coast”, I was really consumed by the idea of the supportive work our team provides startups. Specifically, how it will have to evolve at the risk of us going extinct.

Platform, as loosely defined in my past writings, is the newest function to the venture world and is the one I believe is most at risk with emerging tech. Tools and knowledge are being democratized, and while I view this as an overall positive I think it puts my specific job and the platform category as a whole under pressure. If a founder’s first instinct is to turn to the knowledge of a LLM, where does that leave us?

Founders have always needed - and will always need - two things: talent and customers. It's a bonus if a firm offers anything else. Even when they do, there is added legwork of convincing the founding team to utilize the additional services. At the end of the day, founders will always pause to hear about a new hire recommendation or take a meeting with a potential customer lead.

My hypothesis is that these two areas will remain constant, while productized “AI hacks” for early-stage founders will become a new category of importance. TBD on the uptake for any AI services. All companies are branding as “AI-native” now, so founders may outpace investor AI-fluency (and probably should?), though with some creativity there could still be unique offerings carved out.

My role within the platform team remains a tad murky, but I’m quietly carving out a niche of my own focused on building some GTM/BD initiatives for the fund. I have no significant experience in GTM or BD but I think the most important off-hand advice I received is to think about these two areas in a non-traditional sense. This advice is neither straightforward or profound, but I believe it’s important. Everyone approaches these functions in a very similar way, with community being a horizontal throughput, or trojan horse, for both. People converge on this playbook because you cannot productize GTM or BD when it relates to supporting a generalist portfolio. That unfortunately also implies that anything that is working will likely not be scalable, at least in the beginning.

I’ve noticed that a lot of former athletes go into the BD/Sales space. High-level it makes sense: it’s competitive, you’re mainly reliant on your own work ethic, and there is typically instant gratification of knowing whether you’ve won a deal or not. Timelines vary by industry but this instant knowledge of knowing whether you win or lose is something that draws in former, or current, athletes to roles of this nature. Identifying myself as a former athlete, I can admit to being enticed by this type of work.

Despite the temptation, I’m going to try and approach BD/GTM/whatever-the-hell-it-will-be from a different lens that doesn’t require immediate gratification or results. This makes the actual tracking and metrics of the approach pretty much impossible which is…frustrating. In contrast to the macro startup environment right now that's moving a million miles a minute, the build will be slow. I envision some time of program, maybe one that mirrors a community of sorts, that is consistent and includes people that will actually move the needle for our founders.

Now, on the actual execution, where to start.

Not meant to be a cliffhanger, I’m still genuinely figuring out where to begin. I think the important thing to note is that I have physically begun but with no direction and no formal plan on where it will go or how it will be managed.

I know I’m excited though, and that’s got to count for something.
